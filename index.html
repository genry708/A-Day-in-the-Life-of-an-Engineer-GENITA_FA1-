Morning Routine

My day as an engineer usually starts around 7:00 am. After a quick breakfast, I prepare my plans and check my schedule for the day. I wear my usual collared shirt, pants, and safety shoes, ready to visit the construction site or the office. I often start my morning reviewing blueprints, checking the progress of ongoing projects, and discussing plans with my team.

Some days are spent, surrounded by axhines, workers, and the sound of construction. Other days are quieter. I stay in the office, focusing on deisgns and reports. I like how everyday feels different but always purposeful.

Midday Tasks & Projects

By midday, I'm usually deep into projects. I work closely with architects, technicians, and project managers to mame sure everything follows the plan.

I also use tools like AutoCAD for designs and spreadsheets for analyzing materials and costs. I really like that engineering isn't nust about calculations, it's also about problem-solving. Sometimes, things don't go exactly as planned, but finding solutions is what makes the job rewarding.

A Story from My Dream Job

One story that really inspired me as an aspiring engineer is about Aisa Mijeno, a Filipino engineer who invented the SALT Lamp, a light powered by saltwater. She got the idea when she visited a remote community in Kalinga that didn’t have access to electricity. The people there used kerosene lamps every night, which were not only costly but also dangerous.

Instead of just feeling bad, she decided to do something about it. Using her skills and creativity, she designed a simple lamp that could run on saltwater — something the villagers already had. For me, that’s what being an engineer is all about: finding ways to make people’s lives better using what you know. Her invention might look small, but its impact was huge. It showed me that even a single idea can light up someone’s world — literally.
